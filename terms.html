What Hugin is

Hugin is a workbench for writing open-source drivers for energy devices over Modbus, MQTT, and HTTP. The workbench, the agent, the API, and the driver library are all open source under MIT. Drivers you submit land in a public GitHub repository under your GitHub identity.

What you can expect from us

  • The workbench, agent, and driver library are free to use. No paywall, no per-seat licensing.
  • We pay the AI inference bill. AI usage may be rate-limited or quota-gated; we will be transparent about limits and post them in the workbench.
  • We will not mine your driver work or anything the agent sees on your network. The architecture is designed so we can't — see the Privacy page.
  • We will not change the MIT license on drivers, the agent, or the workbench. Open is the point.
  • We make no commercial guarantees about availability or response time during beta. Best-effort.

What we expect from you

  • Use it on devices you own or have permission to test. Hugin is not a pen-testing tool. Don't sweep networks or probe Modbus on infrastructure you don't control.
  • Don't try to escape the Lua sandbox, abuse the AI quota, or otherwise exploit the system. We will revoke pairing tokens for abuse without warning.
  • Drivers you submit must be MIT-licensable. Don't copy-paste proprietary vendor SDKs or NDA'd register maps. If a register map came from a vendor manual, that's fine — those are reference docs.
  • One pairing, one human. Don't share your GitHub OAuth session.

Safety + liability

Hugin is provided as-is, without warranty. Energy devices can be expensive and physical safety-critical. A buggy driver can damage hardware, trip protection, or corrupt grid registers. Test on disposable hardware first.

Sourceful and any contributor to the open-source repositories are not liable for damage caused by drivers authored with Hugin — whether the driver was written by you, by another contributor, or drafted by AI. This applies to physical damage, lost generation, spoiled batteries, false billing, and downstream control system misbehaviour.

Some jurisdictions don't allow disclaimers this broad. Where that's the case, our liability is limited to the minimum permitted by law, capped at one euro.

Acceptable use

You agree not to use Hugin to:

  • Attack, scan, or interfere with devices, networks, or infrastructure you don't own or have explicit permission to test.
  • Harvest credentials, register maps, or driver source from devices you can't authorize.
  • Reverse-engineer or weaponize the platform itself (the workbench, agent, API, or broker).
  • Submit drivers that knowingly violate device safety limits or local regulations.

Termination

You can stop using Hugin at any time — revoke the GitHub OAuth scope at github.com/settings/applications to disconnect cleanly. We can revoke pairing tokens and NATS credentials at any time for terms violations or abuse, without refund (there's nothing to refund).

Driver PRs you've already merged into srcfl/hugin-drivers remain there under their original MIT licence — termination doesn't retroactively unpublish your contributions.
